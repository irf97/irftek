## The claim being modeled
Mesh impact level is itself a price driver: when the market recognizes a company as
infrastructure for a platform shift, it re-rates in a compressed window. History gives us the
shape and size of those re-ratings. We map each name to an analogue class, scale by its mesh
leverage (c/o flag from the Ledger), and time it with the catalyst spine.

## Monthly path shape (catalyst-stepped, not linear)
Re-ratings arrive in steps at visibility events. Path: **P(m) = S₀ × M^w(m)** where w(m) is
cumulative weight: each of the name's catalyst months carries 3× the weight of a quiet month
(then normalized so w(12)=1). The app's 90-day figure uses k=0.25 (quiet) or **k=0.40 when a
catalyst sits inside 90d** — the pull-forward you named: smart money front-runs the print.
Worked example — NVTS (S₀≈15.4, M=3.0, catalysts Aug+Nov prints, Rubin ramp Q3):
90d ≈ 15.4×3^0.40 ≈ **$24** · 180d ≈ 3^0.65 ≈ **$31** · 365d ≈ **$46** (optimistic path).

## The chain (discipline)
mesh% (Ledger) says **whether it matters** → M (this doc) proposes **how far, optimistically** →
"use as my target" hands it to TGT mode → the **σ-meter grades your audacity** → coverage-weighted
setups price it → **the gate still times it**. The vision never opens a position; it only nominates.

## Honest bounds
Analogue reasoning assumes this wave rhymes with prior infra waves — the one assumption that can
fail wholesale (rate shock, AI-capex pause = every M compresses toward 1). Multipliers are
hand-adjusted judgments on top of class bands; revisit monthly against the journal. Optimistic ≠
median: for a base case, mentally take √M; for bear, the Ledger names don't decouple from the tape.
